Harmonic Microworld

An interactive visualization of pitch-class space — the abstract terrain of musical harmony, stripped of rhythm, timbre, and octave. Play notes with your keyboard, MIDI device, or microphone and watch the geometry of sound unfold in real time.

What is a Microworld?

A microworld is a constrained formal system with its own internal logic — a small universe. Here, the universe contains exactly twelve positions. Their relationships are fixed by interval, not by instrument or notation. The geometry reveals what notation conceals.

Pitch-Class Sets

A pitch class collapses all octaves of a note into one identity: C4, C5, and C2 are all pitch class 0. Allen Forte (1973) catalogued the 208 distinct set-classes that govern atonal music. This tool plots those sets as polygons inscribed in the circle.

The Two Circles

The chromatic circle arranges pitch classes by semitone — physical proximity on a keyboard. The circle of fifths arranges them by perfect fifth — the acoustic foundation of Western tonality. The same notes look different in each geometry.
